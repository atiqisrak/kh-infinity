Import Process

What is the typical timeline for importing goods?

The timeline varies depending on the product type, origin country, and shipping method. Generally:

  • Sea freight: 20-45 days
  • Air freight: 3-7 days
  • Customs clearance: 2-5 business days

What documents are required for importing?

Essential documents include:

  • Commercial Invoice
  • Bill of Lading/Airway Bill
  • Packing List
  • Certificate of Origin
  • Import License (if applicable)
  • Letter of Credit (if required)

Documentation

What are Incoterms and how do they affect my shipment?

Incoterms are international commercial terms that define:

  • Responsibility for shipping costs
  • Insurance requirements
  • Risk transfer points
  • Customs clearance responsibilities
Common terms include FOB, CIF, EXW, and DDP.

Do you help with customs documentation?

Yes, we provide comprehensive customs documentation support:

  • Preparation of all required documents
  • Customs declaration filing
  • HS code classification
  • Compliance verification

We accept various payment methods:

How are shipping costs calculated?

Shipping costs are determined by several factors:

  • Cargo volume and weight
  • Shipping method (air/sea/land)
  • Origin and destination
  • Special handling requirements
  • Insurance coverage
